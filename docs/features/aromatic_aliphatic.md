Aromatic/aliphatic shell features split pocket-lining hydrophobic character into two physically distinct channels by counting aromatic residues (`PHE`, `TYR`, `TRP`, `HIS`) and aliphatic residues (`LEU`, `ILE`, `VAL`, `ALA`, `MET`, `PRO`, `CYS`) in four radial shells around each fpocket centroid. Distances are measured from the pocket centroid to the closest heavy atom of each residue, not to the residue C-alpha, so partially represented fpocket lining residues still contribute signal. The resulting counts and aromatic-to-aliphatic ratios preserve where ring-driven contacts versus dispersion-only hydrophobics sit relative to the pocket core, which makes the exported parquet easier to join onto the Day 1 descriptor table for Day 2 subclass-discrimination experiments.
